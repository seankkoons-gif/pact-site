Constitution (v1)

One tight scope: what counts as evidence, how integrity is verified, and how responsibility is assigned. Governs only generation, verification, and interpretation of Pact evidence—not commercial terms, pricing, or off-chain enforcement.

Rules of Evidence

  • Hash-linked rounds: each round references the previous; chain is unbroken.
  • Signed actors: every negotiation round is signed by participating agents before the next round.
  • Deterministic replay: same inputs and policy produce the same transcript checks.
  • Evidence bundle completeness: transcript, verification report, settlement attempts, and DBL judgment are required.
  • Tamper semantics: any modification breaks the hash chain and is detectable. Verifier outputs FAIL with reason. Tamper that cannot be attributed to a single round is classified as INDETERMINATE_TAMPER.

Why Constitution matters

Makes evidence portable. Reduces subjectivity. Disputes and audits are resolved from cryptographic record, not opinion.

Definitions

  • Transcript: Chronologically ordered, hash-linked sequence of signed negotiation rounds.
  • Evidence Bundle / Auditor Pack: Portable collection: transcript, verification artifacts, DBL judgment, metadata for audit or dispute.
  • Judgment: Deterministic output of Default Blame Logic (DBL): NO_FAULT or RESPONSIBLE_ACTOR (and optionally INDETERMINATE_TAMPER for tamper cases).

Verification requirements

  • Verifier validates all signatures and hash chain integrity.
  • Replay determinism: same inputs → same checks.
  • Output: PASS or FAIL with specific reasons.

Default Blame Logic (DBL)

DBL assigns responsibility using the last valid signed state and policy timing. NO_FAULT when the failure cannot be attributed to an agent; RESPONSIBLE_ACTOR when an agent violated policy, missed timing, or failed a required action. DBL remains deterministic and evidence-constrained.

What the Constitution does not govern

  • Commercial terms, pricing, or off-chain enforcement.
  • Truth of off-chain claims without evidence.
  • Payments or dispute resolution without agreed arbitration policy.
