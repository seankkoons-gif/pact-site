Evidence bundle as the product

Auditor Packs are sealed evidence bundles: transcript, verification report, settlement records, DBL judgment, insurer summary. You can adopt Pact with verifier + packs before running the SDK.

Evidence ≠ Observability. Observability explains what happened; evidence proves it. Pact produces cryptographic evidence—verifiable offline—for audit, disputes, and insurance.

Why it's evidence: Hash-linked rounds · Signed actors · Constitution-scoped verification · Deterministic replay · Tamper-evident bundles

Why Pact exists

Without Pact

  • Logs are informal and mutable
  • Payments prove nothing about intent
  • Failures are ambiguous
  • Disputes require humans and guesswork
  • Insurance and audit are impossible

With Pact

  • Negotiation is signed and hash-linked
  • Intent and policy are explicit
  • Settlement outcomes are deterministic
  • Failures are classified with blame
  • Evidence is portable and verifiable

Pact produces evidence for successful transactions, policy aborts, timeouts, and settlement failures. Failure is a first-class outcome.

What's inside an Auditor Pack

  • Transcript (hash-linked rounds, signatures)
  • Constitution hash + verification report
  • Settlement attempts & receipts
  • DBL judgment artifact
  • Insurer summary JSON + PDF export
  • Tamper detection (hash chain + signatures)

Example: Autonomous API Procurement

An autonomous buyer agent negotiates and purchases an API capability from a provider agent. Pact records the negotiation, settlement attempts, and outcome as verifiable evidence.

Step-by-step flow

  1. Intent — A buyer agent requests access to a paid API capability.
  2. Negotiation — Price, usage limits, and SLA are negotiated across signed, hash-linked rounds.
  3. Policy enforcement — The buyer's policy restricts maximum spend and requires provider identity verification.
  4. Settlement attempt — Payment is attempted through the configured rail.
  5. Outcome — One of three outcomes occurs: Success; Policy Abort (e.g. PACT-101); or Timeout / Non-response (e.g. PACT-404).

Evidence produced

  • Auditor Pack (ZIP) — Full negotiation transcript, verification report, settlement attempt records
  • Offline verification result
  • Default Blame Logic judgment
  • GC-ready PDF + insurer summary
Pact does not determine damages or remedies. It deterministically identifies the responsible actor, or records NO_FAULT when appropriate.

Admissible vs claimed evidence

Admissible: Hash-linked + signed + verifier-checkable. Evidence that can be cryptographically verified against the transcript and policy.

Claimed: Anything without transcript linkage (screenshots, logs, emails). These are assertions without cryptographic proof and are not admissible under Pact's rules of evidence.

Integrity + tamper detection

Any modification to a signed transcript breaks the hash chain and invalidates signatures. Evidence bundles include cryptographic proofs that enable independent verification of integrity.

Evidence references (hashes, signatures, policy matches) are trusted because they can be verified deterministically. Claims without corresponding evidence refs are not admissible.
