When Things Go Wrong

How Pact handles failure without ambiguity or blame games.

Autonomous systems fail in predictable ways. Pact does not prevent failure — it ensures failures produce verifiable evidence and clear responsibility.

Timeline

1

Intent

An agent initiates a request or transaction.

  • Intent is signed
  • Policies are declared
  • Constraints are explicit
2

Negotiation

Terms are negotiated across signed, hash-linked rounds.

  • All offers and counters recorded
  • No silent mutations
  • Deterministic transcript
3

Policy Enforcement

Policies are evaluated before execution.

Possible outcomes: Pass → proceed · Fail → policy abort (e.g. PACT-101)

Policy failures are recorded as evidence, not exceptions.

4

Settlement Attempt

Execution or payment is attempted.

Possible outcomes: Success · Timeout / non-response · Partial fulfillment

All attempts are recorded.

5

Evidence Sealing

An Auditor Pack is generated containing:

  • Full transcript
  • Verification report
  • Settlement records

The pack is sealed and tamper-evident.

6

Verification

Any party can verify the pack offline using the Pact verifier.

Verification checks: Hash chain integrity · Signatures · Constitution compliance · Deterministic replay

7

Responsibility Attribution

Default Blame Logic produces a signed judgment:

  • RESPONSIBLE_ACTOR, or
  • NO_FAULT

This judgment is evidence — not an opinion.

8

Review & Resolution

Evidence can be reviewed by: Legal · Risk · Insurers · Auditors

Pact does not resolve disputes. It makes resolution possible.

Failures are inevitable. Ambiguity is optional.
