Pact: Evidence for Insurers and Underwriters

Assess autonomous system risk using verifiable proof, not operator claims.

Download Insurer Overview (PDF)

The Underwriting Problem

Autonomous agents can transact, negotiate, and execute without human review. When failures occur, insurers are often presented with incomplete logs, subjective explanations, or platform-controlled dashboards.

This makes underwriting, claims evaluation, and post-incident review unreliable.

What Pact Provides

Pact produces sealed evidence bundles ("Auditor Packs") that insurers can independently verify offline.

Each pack contains:

  • A complete, hash-linked negotiation transcript
  • Signed identities for all participating agents
  • Policy checks and settlement attempts
  • A verification report confirming integrity
  • A signed responsibility attribution (Default Blame Logic)
  • A concise insurer summary

No access to Pact infrastructure or the originating platform is required.

Pact evidence does not rely on platform assertions or operator-controlled telemetry.

Responsibility Attribution

Pact deterministically identifies:

  • A responsible actor, or
  • NO_FAULT

This attribution is based solely on signed protocol evidence and published rules.

Pact does not assess damages, liability limits, or coverage decisions.

Coverage determinations remain solely the responsibility of the insurer.

Why This Matters for Insurers

With Pact:

  • Claims can be evaluated using portable, replayable evidence
  • Responsibility is provable, not disputed
  • Evidence remains valid even if platforms or vendors change
  • Risk models can be built on real failure data, not anecdotes

What Pact Is Not

Pact is not:

  • An arbitration system
  • A claims processor
  • A custodian of funds
  • A black-box scoring model

It is an evidence standard.

Pact allows insurers to underwrite autonomous systems using verifiable evidence, not post-incident explanations.
